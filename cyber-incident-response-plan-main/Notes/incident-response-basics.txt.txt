PHASE 1: Understanding Incident Response

A cyber incident refers to any event that threatens or violates the confidentiality, integrity, or availability (CIA) of an organizationâ€™s systems, networks, or data. Examples include ransomware attacks, phishing campaigns, malware infections, unauthorized system access, and data breaches.

A Cyber Security Incident Management Plan is a structured and organized approach that defines how an organization detects, responds to, manages, and recovers from cybersecurity incidents. The purpose of this plan is to minimize operational disruption, reduce financial and reputational damage, and ensure a coordinated response during security emergencies.

Without a clear response strategy, organizations may face extended downtime, data loss, financial penalties, and serious damage to their reputation. A well-developed incident response plan ensures that actions are taken quickly, systematically, and effectively.
